# French-knowledge-of-US-holidays
French knowledge of American holidays: 61% average score, strong generational gaps. Data from 40 respondents.

## Abstract

This study examines French knowledge of three major American holidays 
(Halloween, Thanksgiving, Christmas) through a 20-question quiz administered 
to 40 individuals. Results show an average score of 61% (12.2/20) with 
significant generational gaps: younger respondents (12-18 years) scored 
73% while older respondents (65+) scored 52%. Women slightly outperformed 
men (62.9% vs 59%). While mainstream cultural elements were widely known 
(100% knew Halloween's date), historical aspects showed low recognition 
(10-22.5%). The findings suggest broad but superficial knowledge of 
American holidays in France, influenced by media and education rather 
than deep cultural understanding.

## Project Overview
Analysis of French knowledge about American holidays (Halloween, Thanksgiving, Christmas) through a 20-question quiz administered to 40 individuals.

## Key Findings
- Average score: 12.2/20 (61%)
- Generational gap: 21-point difference between youngest (73%) and oldest (52%)
- Gender difference: Women slightly outperform men (62.9% vs 59%)
- Cultural knowledge: Mainstream elements well-known, historical aspects poorly recognized

## Research Questions
1. How well do French people know major American holidays?
2. Are there significant differences by age or gender?
3. Which aspects (dates, symbols, history) are best/worst known?

## Methodology
- Sample: 40 respondents (21 women, 19 men)
- Age range: 7 to 65+ years
- Data collection: December 2024 via phone/in-person
- Analysis: Descriptive statistics, comparative analysis
